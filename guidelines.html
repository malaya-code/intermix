Guiding Principles

Hosting and Participation

  Anyone is welcome to propose a session for Intermix. Sessions are participant-led and centered on a single piece of media. Support is available for anyone who would like help preparing or co-facilitating a session. If you are interested in hosting, you can use the Intermix Media Submission Form. Host a session ?

Accessibility for Everyone

(inside and outside of academia)

  Intermix is a discussion group for adults interested in engaging with media and ideas across different disciplines.   Participation does not require academic credentials or prior expertise. Sessions are designed to be accessible to people with varied backgrounds, experience levels, and approaches to learning.   The goal is to make participation feel possible without requiring performance, self-justification, or demonstrations of knowledge.

What to Expect

 
  • Each session centers on a single piece of media chosen by one participant.
  • Discussion may include analysis, reaction, questioning, or contextual exploration.
  • A range of interpretations and viewpoints is expected.
  • Participants may engage actively, minimally, or primarily through listening.

What Not To Expected Here

 
  • Academic training or formal disciplinary background
  • Prior familiarity with the material
  • Agreement or consensus
  • A specific level or style of participation

Guiding Principles for Interaction

  Intermix values engagement that is thoughtful, respectful, and grounded in shared curiosity.   Participants are expected to treat differences in knowledge, background, or experience with care. Dismissive or belittling behavior is not acceptable.   Boundaries should be respected when stated. Critique, correction, or advice should be offered only when invited and should remain focused on ideas rather than individuals.

What Is Not Okay Here

  Harassment, bullying, or discriminatory behavior is not tolerated. This includes behavior that targets or excludes participants based on identity, background, discipline, or perceived level of knowledge.   Conduct that undermines a respectful discussion environment may result in removal from the space.

Questions?

  Have a question about accessibility, facilitation, or constructive criticism? You can email intermix[underscore]club@proton[dot]me.
